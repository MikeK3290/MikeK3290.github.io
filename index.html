The Acoustic guitar emerged from spain in the early 16th century, deriving from the guitarra latina.

The Acoustic guitars are traditionally made with wood with a hollow body and a circular opening. The sound emerges from the opening when the strings are strummed.

Acoustic guitars are made a numerous types of wood such as Maple, Koa, Sapele, Spruce, Sitka spruce, Mahogany, Western red cedar, Cedar, Rosewood and many more. Acoustic guitars do not need technology to operate, unlike electronic guitars. All the energy is provided by the plucking of the string. So, no external energy is needed increase the loudness of the sound.

The body of an acoustic guitar consists of the soundboard( or top) plus the back and sides.

The neck of the guitar projects from the main guitar body and consists of a number of pieces and parts. Necks can have different shapes, such as being a V shape to being a C shape and every point in between. The quality of the instrument is determinded by how rigid the guitarâ€™s neck is. However, bending the neck somwhat to change the pitch of a note can be desirable for certain kinds of genre.

On the front of the neck is the fingerboard, by far one of the most important parts to consider when selecting a guitar, as it affects comfort, playing style, and tone. It is generally made from a different type of wood as the back of the neck and is fretted. Ebony, rosewood, and maple are typical woods used, though synthetics such as Richlite are another option.

Guitar frets are raised parts on the fingerboard of the guitar that extend across the full width of the neck, and are generally made of metal. Pressing the string against the fret shortens the string and thereby changing the note and making it easier to achieve correct notes than a non-fretted fingerboard. Frets come in different shapes and types, but will wear over time, which makes fret maintenance important.

Guitars often have dots or custom marks inlaid into the fretboard which can serve as a visual aid to users.
